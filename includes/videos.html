MMP: Main Idea

Suppose the "pseudo-moments" $\color{pink}\mu_{\alpha}$ obtained from the SDP satisfy \begin{align*} E_{\color{pink}\mu}[u_i^2] &= (E_{\color{pink}\mu}[u_i])^2\\ E_{\color{pink}\mu}[v_i^2] &= (E_{\color{pink}\mu}[v_i])^2, \quad i = 1,\ldots,s \end{align*} Then, $\color{pink}\mu$ is a geniune distribution.


Idea: Let's "push" our solution to satisfy this condition.

First attempt: add \[\color{yellow}\lambda(u_1, v_1, \ldots) := \sum_{i} E_{\color{pink}\mu}[u_i^2] - (E_{\color{pink}\mu}[u_i])^2 + E_{\color{pink}\mu}[v_i^2] - (E_{\color{pink}\mu}[v_i])^2\] to our objective.

More refined approach:

  • Start with randomly initialized "pseudo-moments" $\color{pink}\mu^{(1)} = \{\color{pink}\mu^{(1)}_{\alpha}\}$.
  • Linearize $\color{yellow}\lambda$ around $\color{pink}\mu{(1)}$.
  • Solve the corresponding SDP to obtain $\color{pink}\mu^{(2)}$
  • Linearize $\color{yellow}\lambda$ around $\color{pink}\mu{(2)}$.
  • Solve the corresponding SDP to obtain $\color{pink}\mu^{(3)}$
  • $\cdots$
